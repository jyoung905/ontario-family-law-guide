# Design System Specification: The Editorial Sanctuary

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
This design system is built to transform the often-cold, clinical experience of legal navigation into a journey of dignified clarity. We are moving away from the "Law Firm Template" and toward a **"High-End Editorial Sanctuary."**

**Creative North Star: The Curated Haven**
The UI should feel like a premium printed journal—spacious, authoritative, yet deeply human. We achieve this by breaking the rigid, boxy constraints of traditional apps through intentional asymmetry, overlapping elements, and a "Tonal Layering" approach to depth. This system prioritizes the user's emotional state, using soft textures and a warm, low-contrast base to reduce cognitive load during stressful legal transitions.

---

## 2. Colors & Surface Architecture

### The Palette
Our palette balances the "Expert" (Deep Midnight Teal) with the "Human" (Warm Sand).

*   **Primary (`#002631`):** Use for high-authority elements. This is the voice of the expert.
*   **Surface (`#fcf9f2`):** Our primary background. It is a warm, breathable sand that prevents the sterile "white-screen fatigue."
*   **Tertiary/CTA (`#450e09` / `#62231b`):** This grounded coral/tertiary tone is used for path-finding and primary actions. It is vibrant enough to grab attention but muted enough to feel sophisticated.

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Explicit Instruction:** Designers are prohibited from using 1px solid borders to define sections or containers. 
Structure must be created through:
1.  **Background Shifts:** Transitioning from `surface` to `surface-container-low`.
2.  **Vertical Space:** Utilizing the spacing scale to create distinct content groupings.
3.  **Tonal Transitions:** Defining an area by its container color, not its edge.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the interface as a physical desk of stacked high-quality paper.
*   **Base Layer:** `surface` (The foundation).
*   **Section Layer:** `surface-container-low` (Large content blocks).
*   **Component Layer:** `surface-container-lowest` (Cards or interactive elements that need to "lift" off the page).
*   **Nesting:** An inner container should always be at least one tier "brighter" or "lower" than its parent to define hierarchy without lines.

### The Glass & Gradient Rule
To provide a signature feel, floating elements (modals, navigation bars) should utilize **Glassmorphism**. Use `surface` colors at 85% opacity with a `backdrop-blur` of 12px–20px. This allows the organic background textures to bleed through, softening the UI’s footprint.

---

## 3. Typography: The Editorial Voice

We utilize a high-contrast typographic scale to create an "Editorial" feel.

*   **The Authority (Display & Headline):** `Newsreader`. A premium serif that conveys empathy and tradition. 
    *   *Implementation:* Use `display-lg` for welcome screens and `headline-md` for section starts. Keep tracking tight and line-height comfortable (1.2).
*   **The Clarity (Title & Body):** `Inter`. A clean, modern sans-serif for high-density information.
    *   *Implementation:* Body text should never be pure black; use `on-surface-variant` for a softer, more readable contrast.
*   **The Utility (Labels):** `Manrope`. Used for functional micro-copy. Use `label-md` in all-caps with slightly increased letter spacing (0.05em) for a sophisticated, "tagged" appearance.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth

### The Layering Principle
Avoid traditional "Box Shadows." Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering**. Place a `surface-container-lowest` card on a `surface-container` background. The subtle shift from `#ffffff` to `#f1eee7` creates a natural, soft lift that feels integrated rather than floating.

### Ambient Shadows
When a true floating effect is required (e.g., a primary CTA button or a critical modal):
*   **Color:** Use a tinted version of `on-surface` (never pure black).
*   **Diffusion:** Shadows must be extra-diffused. Example: `offset: 0px 8px, blur: 24px, opacity: 6%`.
*   **The Ghost Border:** If a boundary is required for accessibility, use `outline-variant` at 15% opacity. Never use a 100% opaque border.

---

## 5. Components

### Buttons
*   **Primary:** Uses `primary-container` (`#003d4d`) with `on-primary` text. Shape: `xl` (1.5rem) or `full`.
*   **Secondary:** Glassmorphic style. `surface` at 50% opacity with a `ghost border` (10% `outline`).
*   **Signature Interaction:** Buttons should have a subtle "organic" swell on hover, rather than a simple color change.

### Cards & Lists
*   **Forbid Dividers:** Do not use horizontal lines between list items. Use 16px–24px of vertical padding and `surface-container` shifts to differentiate items.
*   **Asymmetric Cards:** Experiment with slightly off-center text alignment within cards (e.g., 24px left padding, 48px right padding) to break the "grid" feel.

### Input Fields
*   **Style:** Minimalist. No bottom line or heavy box. Use `surface-container-high` as a subtle background fill with `md` (0.75rem) rounded corners.
*   **Focus State:** Transition the background to `surface-container-highest` and apply a soft `primary` tinted glow.

### Signature Component: The "Guided Progress"
A custom-styled, organic line-art progress tracker. Use `tertiary` for the active path. The lines should feel hand-drawn or slightly imperfect to reinforce the "human-centered" brand personality.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
*   **Embrace the Off-Center:** Use asymmetrical layouts for hero sections. A headline aligned to the 1/3 mark of the screen feels more "custom" than a centered one.
*   **Use Generous White Space:** If you think there is enough padding, add 16px more. Space is the ultimate luxury in a legal app.
*   **Use Subtle Grain:** Apply a 2% opacity grain texture to the `surface` layer to mimic the feel of tactile paper.

### Don't:
*   **Don't use "Legal Blue":** Avoid standard #0000FF or saturated corporate blues. Stick to the Midnight Teal (`primary`).
*   **Don't use sharp corners:** Nothing in this app should feel "sharp." Use the `md` to `xl` roundedness scale for everything.
*   **Don't use generic icons:** Avoid standard Material or FontAwesome glyphs. Use custom thin-line art icons that feel illustrative and warm.
*   **Don't center-align long-form text:** Keep body text left-aligned for maximum readability and a professional editorial look.

---
**Director's Note:** 
Remember, we are building a "Sanctuary." Every pixel should contribute to a sense of calm. If a design element feels "loud" or "rigid," simplify it. Let the typography and the warmth of the palette do the heavy lifting.```
