Please review and improve the Ontario Family Law Guide app based on the following product/UX audit.

Important context:
- This app is for self-represented people in Ontario family court.
- The users are often stressed, confused, and unfamiliar with legal language.
- The app already has a strong concept and should NOT be rebuilt from scratch.
- The goal is to make the flow clearer, smoother, more confidence-building, and more conversion-friendly.
- Keep the tone calm, plain-language, supportive, and trustworthy.
- Do not make it sound like a law firm or legal representation service.

Main product issue:
The biggest problem is not design polish, it is flow confidence. A stressed user must feel at each step:
- I am in the right place
- I understand what this means
- I know what happens next
- I can recover if I am unsure

Please implement the following:

1. Simplify the landing/start screen
- Reduce cognitive load
- Reduce text density
- Make the first choice easier and calmer
- Do not let free vs premium compete too strongly with the main path selection
- Keep the main three entry options, but make them more scannable and easier to understand

2. Improve the three entry pathways
Current pathways:
- I was served with papers
- I’m starting a case
- I have an existing case

Please:
- make each option easier to understand in plain English
- add short helper descriptions/examples under each
- make it clear what kind of user should choose each one

3. Add stronger uncertainty handling throughout the app
This is critical.
Users often do not know:
- what documents they received
- what stage they are at
- what terms mean
- whether they are choosing the right branch

Please add:
- “Not sure” options where appropriate
- reassurance that rough answers are okay
- small helper text for legal terms
- “What does this mean?” expandable explanations where useful

4. Add branch confirmation after initial triage
After the user answers the first key questions, show a short confirmation screen or summary such as:
- what the app thinks their situation is
- what it will help them do next
- why this path was chosen

The user should feel guided, not silently routed.

5. Break down the “I have an existing case” branch
This path is too broad and likely confusing.
Please split it into narrower, more specific subpaths such as:
- I need to respond to something
- I already filed and have a next step coming up
- I need help serving or filing something
- I’m not sure what stage my case is at

6. Improve progress/orientation throughout the experience
On each screen, the user should understand:
- what this step is for
- what happens next
- how many steps are left
- whether they can go back safely

Please strengthen:
- progress indicators
- step purpose copy
- navigation confidence
- orientation messaging

7. Delay or soften premium pressure until after first value
The app currently shows free vs premium early.
Please keep premium visible, but make sure the user first feels:
- understood
- oriented
- somewhat relieved
before stronger premium pressure appears

Premium should feel like:
- now I want more help
not:
- pay before I trust this

8. Rewrite premium feature descriptions to be outcome-based
Current feature names may be too abstract.
Please rewrite them into more concrete, user-centered outcomes.

Example direction:
Instead of abstract labels, use language like:
- draft the forms you likely need
- organize your affidavit chronology
- prepare a clearer filing package
- get step-by-step help with what to say and send

9. Add scope clarity early
Users may not know whether the app is for their exact situation.
Please add a short section or helper text explaining:
- what the app helps with
- what it does not currently cover
- that it is plain-language guidance, not legal representation

10. Audit for wrong-path risk
Please review all branching logic and UX for places where a confused user could:
- choose the wrong path
- get stuck
- lose trust
- fail to recover cleanly

Where possible:
- add recovery paths
- allow easy backtracking
- add confirmation before major branch commitments

Implementation goals:
- smoother first-run experience
- more confidence for stressed users
- clearer legal-language translation
- stronger guidance without overcomplicating the app
- better conversion from free user to premium user through trust and clarity

Please make the changes directly in the app, not just suggest them.

After making the changes, give me:
1. a concise changelog
2. any remaining weak points or open questions
3. any parts of the flow that still need product decisions from me
